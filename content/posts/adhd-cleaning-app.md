If you have ever downloaded a cleaning app, used it for three days, then abandoned it in shame — this post is for you.

Most cleaning apps are built for neurotypical brains. They give you a rigid schedule, send guilt notifications when you are overdue, and treat every day the same. If you have ADHD: **every day is not the same.**

## What Makes a Good ADHD Cleaning App?

**Meet you where you are.** Ask two questions before giving tasks: how much energy (1-5) and how much time? A 1-energy 15-minute plan looks nothing like a 4-energy 60-minute plan.

**Break things into tiny pieces.** Clean the kitchen is not a task — it is a project. A good app breaks it into: clear the left counter (2 min), put dishes in the sink (1 min), wipe the stovetop (2 min).

**Have a panic button.** Guests arriving in 20 minutes? Speed-clean mode, prioritized by what guests see.

**Never use guilt.** Shame freezes us. Good apps track effort, not perfection.
